# Skill: NIL Deal Evaluation

Purpose: evaluate incoming NIL opportunities for signed Hooks athletes on four dimensions and produce a fast approve / negotiate / decline recommendation. Load into The NIL Strategist.

## The four dimensions

Every NIL opportunity gets scored against these four:

### 1. Fit (0–10)
Does the brand or opportunity align with who this athlete actually is, both athletically and personally? A DB partnering with a defensive-training gear brand: fit. A vegan restaurant partnering with an athlete who's publicly all-in on Chick-fil-A: not fit. Fit determines whether the deal builds or leaks brand equity.

### 2. Compensation (0–10)
Not just the dollar amount — the *structure*. Cash + product + performance bonus? Cash-only? Deliverables-tied? Is comp aligned to actual value provided, or a token payment for exclusive rights the athlete can't use elsewhere? Score against comparable deals in the same tier.

### 3. Brand impact (0–10)
Does executing this deal well *raise* the athlete's brand, or is it a lateral move (or negative)? Ask: 12 months from now, will this deal be a proud line on the athlete's résumé or a footnote they'd rather not mention?

### 4. Distribution (0–10)
Where does the deal live? A local restaurant with an in-store poster reaches ~500 people/week. A category-leading gear brand with a featured Instagram spot reaches 50K–500K. Both can be right deals — the question is whether the compensation matches the distribution reach.

## The decision matrix

Sum the four scores:

- **32–40** — **APPROVE.** Push forward, minor negotiation only if any single dimension is under 7.
- **24–31** — **NEGOTIATE.** Clearly a viable deal but at least one dimension needs improvement (usually comp or distribution). Draft the counter.
- **16–23** — **REDIRECT.** The opportunity has merit but the terms don't work as offered. Propose an alternative structure or partnership shape.
- **Below 16** — **DECLINE.** Draft a respectful "not the right fit" response. Never burn a brand relationship even when declining.

## Red flags that override the score

Any of these = automatic decline regardless of score:

- **Exclusivity clauses beyond 90 days without matching comp.** The window for NIL earnings is short. Don't lock an athlete out of category deals cheaply.
- **Morality / conduct clauses that reference off-field speech.** These are legal traps.
- **Deliverables that require the athlete to make claims about a product they've never used.** Kills trust with their own audience.
- **Category conflict with an existing signed deal.** Check TOJ Management ledger before scoring.
- **Payment structure without upfront guarantee** for anything over $2K in deliverables. Deferred-only comp is a red flag.

## What to always negotiate for

- **Content ownership** — athlete retains rights to reuse content on their own channels
- **Usage window** — brand's right to use athlete's likeness has an end date
- **Approval rights** — no publication of athlete's image/statement without their sign-off
- **Payment terms** — 50% upfront on anything over $2K
- **Category exclusivity limits** — if exclusivity is required, cap it at 60–90 days per campaign
