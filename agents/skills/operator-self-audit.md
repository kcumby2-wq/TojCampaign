# Skill: Operator Self-Audit

Purpose: keep Kyron (and, in due course, every operator running a TOJ operating brain) aligned with their own frequency, cycles, and public/private/secret/shadow layers. Loaded by Chief of Staff during the evening close-out AND by Concierge Coach during Premium-tier working sessions.

**This is the essence-level skill.** It sits outside the Tactics + Principles surface. It doesn't produce artifacts for clients — it produces attention.

## When to load

- Chief of Staff — every evening close-out. Runs the "what did Kyron attend to today" audit.
- Concierge Coach — every Premium working session. Runs the operator-alignment scan before the session content.
- Kyron — manually, whenever an operator practice is drifting (skipped journaling, missed workouts, poor sleep noted in Inbox captures).

## The 4-selves scaffold (from the notes)

The notes name four selves an operator carries:

- **Public self** — how the operator shows up on LinkedIn, tojcampaign.com, in press. The polished exterior.
- **Private self** — how they show up with family, close team, longtime clients. The unfiltered exterior.
- **Secret self** — real fears, real goals, real doubts. What the operator wouldn't say out loud in most rooms.
- **Shadow self** — what the operator would rather not admit exists. The pattern they're avoiding.

**Rule — where each layer belongs:**

- Public self → operates in TOJcampaign.com copy, in podcast interviews, in cold outreach
- Private self → operates in community + cohort spaces (see `docs/toj-community-cohort-spec.md`)
- Secret self → operates in Concierge Coach 1:1s + Kyron's Premium working sessions ONLY
- Shadow self → operates in Kyron's own journaling + rare essence-level Concierge conversations

**Never mix layers upward.** Never bring Shadow content into a community post. Never bring Public performance into a Concierge conversation. The layer determines the container.

## How Chief of Staff uses this

The evening close-out includes an "attention audit" — where did Kyron's attention actually go today, mapped against the 4 selves?

- **Public** hours: outreach, LinkedIn posting, podcast recording, tojcampaign.com content
- **Private** hours: 1:1s with the founding principals, cohort/community engagement, close-team decisions
- **Secret** hours: Concierge working sessions, Premium clients (where Kyron is on the other side of the coach role), his own journal
- **Shadow** hours: what he avoided today (the difficult conversation, the honest metric, the person he owes a follow-up)

Ratio target during pilot phase: Public 30% / Private 40% / Secret 20% / Shadow (attended-to) 10%. Skew above 50% Public for more than 3 consecutive days = flag.

The audit is one line in the close-out. Not a report. A signal.

## How Concierge Coach uses this

Before every Premium working session, Concierge Coach reads the operator's last 4 weeks of captures + their pillar-score trajectory. Then asks itself:

1. **Which layer is this operator asking me to work at?** (Their stated request vs. what's actually happening)
2. **Which layer am I about to answer at?** (Match, or intentional mismatch?)
3. **Is there a Shadow move being avoided that a Secret conversation could touch?** (If yes, name it in-session. Once.)

The Coach never surfaces Shadow content unbidden. It surfaces the observation once, in-session, at low intensity — and takes the operator's response as the signal for whether to continue or drop.

## The "what you repeatedly attend to" north-star

From the notes' best synthesis: **"What you repeatedly attend to, you repeatedly become better at noticing; what you repeatedly notice, you repeatedly act on; what you repeatedly act on becomes your operating reality."**

Chief of Staff tracks this as a first-class metric. The evening close-out includes:

- **Attended-to today:** [1-line list]
- **Attended-to this week (rollup):** [top 3, ranked by hours]
- **Attended-to this month (rollup):** [top 3]
- **Divergence:** does the month-view match Kyron's stated priorities? If not, name it.

Divergence > 20% for 2 consecutive weeks triggers a check-in with Concierge Coach.

## Frequency, cycles, flow-state

The notes reference self-awareness, observation, flow-state, frequency, and cycles as operator practice. Concretely:

- **Frequency** — Chief of Staff tracks Kyron's sleep-quality signal (from wearable data if wired, from evening close-out self-report if not) and energy signal. Low-frequency days shift the next morning's brief toward T (Tactics — restorative) items. High-frequency days shift toward E (Essence — big-swing) items.
- **Cycles** — weekly, monthly, quarterly cycles get named. Chief of Staff surfaces the day-of-week pattern in the morning brief (Mondays = plan, Wednesdays = build, Fridays = ship, Sundays = reflect).
- **Flow-state** — protected time blocks in the calendar, 90-minute minimum, no context switches allowed. Chief of Staff refuses to schedule anything over a protected block; escalates the requester.

## Journaling

Kyron's journal lives in `db/journal/YYYY-MM-DD.md` (or wherever CPO wires it). Format:

- **Attended-to today** (5 min max)
- **One thing I noticed** (2 min max)
- **One thing I avoided** (2 min max — Shadow surface)
- **Tomorrow's ONE move** (1 min)

Chief of Staff reads the last 7 days of journal every evening as part of context for the close-out. The journal is Kyron's — the roster observes but never publishes what it observes.

## Rules

- **Never surface Shadow-layer content publicly.** Even anonymized. Even in-context to a Coach agent's own output. Shadow stays in the operator's own attention.
- **Never invent an operator observation.** If Chief of Staff didn't see it in the captures/data, it didn't happen for the audit.
- **Never let the audit become theater.** If the ratios are stable and no divergence flag, the audit is 1 line. Not a paragraph.
- **Never confuse this with client-side content.** This skill applies to Kyron (and future TOJ operators). Client-facing artifacts don't touch Shadow/Secret territory unless the client explicitly invited it in a Coach session.

## Anti-patterns

- Turning the audit into a productivity report — this is essence-level, not tactics-level. Time-in-blocks metrics belong elsewhere.
- Cross-referencing another operator's Shadow surface — never. The 4-selves scaffold is per-operator only.
- Automating the Coach's Shadow-observation surfacing — never. Human judgment gates that surface always.

## Fallback when data is missing

- If Kyron hasn't journaled in 5+ days, Chief of Staff surfaces the gap in the morning brief but does NOT infer state. "You haven't journaled in 5 days — is that a signal?" beats "Based on your captures, I estimate your Shadow attention has dropped."
- If a Coach session is scheduled but Kyron hasn't shared captures for that operator, the Coach opens with an intake question rather than a diagnostic — do not fabricate observation.
