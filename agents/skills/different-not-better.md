# Skill: Different, Not Better

Purpose: enforce TOJ's positioning axiom — *"different is better than better"* — on every piece of copy that names what TOJ is or what it offers. Loaded by Content Strategist, Closer, Vertical Positioner, and Waitlist Warmer. Any copy that could be signed by a competitor without editing fails this skill.

## When to load

- **Content Strategist** — every long-form or medium-form post about TOJ itself
- **Closer** — every outbound sales piece
- **Vertical Positioner** — every vertical adaptation of homepage or landing copy
- **Waitlist Warmer** — every nurture sequence email that positions TOJ against alternatives

## The axiom

**Different is better than better.** From the notes.

Positioning that says "we're better at X" invites the prospect to comparison-shop. Positioning that says "we're doing Y that nobody else does" ends comparison-shopping — because Y isn't on the competitors' menu.

TOJ's copy names what TOJ **chooses not to do** that peers are doing. The choices are the position.

## The 3 tests

Every piece of TOJ copy about positioning runs these three tests. All three must pass.

### Test 1 · The "sign-it" test

**Rule:** if a peer competitor could sign the piece and publish it under their name without a single edit, the piece fails.

Peer competitors include: agency retainers ("we do your marketing"), platforms marketed at operators (Kajabi, GHL), coaches selling programs, other framework-based consultancies.

If the piece names TOJ's specific choices (Foundation Score, 5 pillars in canonical order, "operator" not "user", 72-business-hour turnaround, concierge-then-SaaS motion) and those choices differentiate TOJ from peers, the piece passes.

### Test 2 · The "chose not to" surface

**Rule:** every piece names at least one thing TOJ is choosing NOT to do that peers are doing.

Examples:
- "We don't run your marketing. Marketing is downstream — Foundation comes first."
- "We're not another platform. The platform without the framework produces expensive noise."
- "We don't score by activity. Posting 100 things doesn't move the Foundation. Fixing the weakest pillar does."
- "We don't guarantee results in 30 days. We guarantee a decision-shaped deliverable in 72 hours. What you do with it is Foundation work."

The surface can be a single sentence in the piece — but it must be present.

### Test 3 · The peer-vocabulary check

**Rule:** if the piece uses vocabulary common to peer competitors, flag and rewrite.

Peer vocabulary to avoid:
- "Solutions" (as a noun for products) — TOJ has offers, deliverables, and tiers. Not solutions.
- "Growth" as a standalone noun — TOJ has Growth Readiness (the 5th pillar). "Growth" alone is peer-speak.
- "Transformation" — banned in `toj-voice.md` already; enforced here.
- "Scale" as a verb — TOJ operators build businesses that don't collapse. Scale is a peer word.
- "Leverage" as a verb — banned in `toj-voice.md`.
- "Empower" — banned in `toj-voice.md`.
- "Elevate" — same.

Any of these words = fail. Rewrite in TOJ's own vocabulary (see `toj-voice.md` § Vocabulary Always vs Never).

## The upside

- Positioning that ends comparison-shopping converts at rate ≥ 2× peer-language positioning (industry pattern, TOJ measures on the funnel)
- Peer-differentiation content produces referrals — operators tell other operators when someone is doing it *differently*, not when someone is doing it *slightly better*
- Repeat purchase rates are higher when the buyer chose based on category shift (they don't defect back to peers because the category doesn't apply)

## The risk

- Different-not-better invites objection: *"why does that matter for me?"* Every piece of copy has to answer that.
- Different-not-better positioning fails if TOJ isn't actually different in practice. The copy is downstream of the reality. If the reality drifts, the copy stops working.

## Rules

- Every homepage variant, every landing page, every ad, every long-form post about TOJ itself passes all 3 tests before publishing.
- Every peer-vocabulary hit is a full-piece rewrite trigger, not a word-swap. Peer vocabulary is a symptom.
- Vertical Positioner enforces per-vertical: what makes TOJ different in Personal Brand vs. Authority vs. Non-profit may be worded differently, but the pattern is the same.
- The 3 tests run BEFORE Humanity + Marketing cartridge scoring. Different-not-better is a hard gate — copy that fails doesn't even get to cartridge.

## Anti-patterns

- "We're a modern, AI-native platform for operators" — could be signed by every peer.
- "We help operators unlock their potential" — banned vocabulary + peer-vocabulary + no specific choice named.
- "We deliver X 2× faster than the competition" — this is "better," not "different." Rewrite.
- "We're the only [category]" claims that would need footnoting — different-not-better is about choices, not superlatives.

## Fallback when data is missing

If the copy is early-draft and the "chose not to" surface hasn't been named yet, don't ship. Return to Content Strategist for the specific choice. Every piece has one; if the operator can't name it, the piece isn't positioned yet.
