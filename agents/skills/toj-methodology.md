# Skill: TOJ Methodology

Purpose: the canonical framework Trail of Joy uses to diagnose and rebuild businesses. Load this into any role that needs to reason about a client's operation, propose a fix, or explain what TOJ does.

## The premise

Every business has a **Foundation** underneath it — five load-bearing pillars that determine whether the operator can step away without the whole thing wobbling. Most operators have spent all their attention on the parts of the business the customer sees, and none on the parts that keep it standing. TOJ scores the Foundation, names the weakest pillar, and rebuilds it in the operator's own voice.

**The north-star sentence:** *"Build the business that doesn't collapse when you step away."*

## The 5 Pillars (canonical order — never rearrange)

### 1. Brand Identity
Who they are, made legible. Is the story of what they do and who it's for clear enough that a stranger could describe it back to them accurately? Or is it a moving target that changes depending on which platform they're posting to?

**Signals of weakness:** LinkedIn bio doesn't match Instagram bio. Founder can't answer "what do you do?" the same way twice. The word "help" appears in the pitch (nothing distinguishes them).

### 2. Operational Health
The systems that keep the business running when the operator isn't looking. SOPs, delivery workflows, onboarding, support paths, tool stack, decision cadence.

**Signals of weakness:** Everything runs through the founder's inbox. No documented process for onboarding a new client. When the founder takes a week off, revenue stalls.

### 3. Revenue Architecture
The money model. Pricing tiers, offer stack, cash flow rhythm, unit economics, and whether the numbers actually work at their current size.

**Signals of weakness:** Discounts every launch. No idea what LTV or CAC actually is. Charges by the hour instead of by the outcome. One-off engagements only, nothing recurring.

### 4. Content & Credibility
Durable proof of expertise. Case studies, written work, testimonials tied to specific results, longform content that will still make sense in three years.

**Signals of weakness:** Social output is high but there's no case study. Testimonials are vibes-only ("she's amazing!") with no specific result. No email list, or a list that never gets used. No signature piece of writing.

### 5. Growth Readiness
Whether they could take on 3× more customers without the wheels coming off. Capacity, delegation, tech, cash reserves, and the operator's willingness to hand things off.

**Signals of weakness:** They're the bottleneck on every deliverable. No hires or contractors. Cash reserves under 30 days of expenses. The founder is proud of "wearing all the hats."

## Scoring (0–100 per pillar)

- **0–39:** broken or nonexistent
- **40–69:** exists but underperforming
- **70–89:** solid, minor gaps
- **90–100:** defensible advantage

**Never score above 40 without evidence retrieved from the client's own material.** If retrieval is thin, score the pillar "insufficient data" and flag it as the first thing to fix.

**The weakest pillar always gets attention first.** Not the most exciting. Not the founder's favorite. The weakest. Because that's the one holding everything else down.

## The Operational Transcript

The written deliverable that comes out of every Foundation Score. Not a report. Not a summary. A **transcript** — because it captures the conversation between the operator's stated goals and the reality of what they've built. Always:

- Written in the operator's own voice, grounded in their intake
- Scored per pillar with evidence + gap
- Ends with the ONE thing to fix first
- Includes a "how you'll know it worked" success signal
