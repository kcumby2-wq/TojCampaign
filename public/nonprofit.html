We install the operating system that turns program energy into reportable, repeatable, fundable work — without changing what makes your mission yours.

What most nonprofits lose in year three

Every new program was supposed to feel like the first one. It doesn’t.

“Mission preserved. At scale.”

The first program worked because founders were in every room. The second worked because they were in most of them. By the fourth, the mission is being interpreted through people who have never met the founders — and the drift starts.

We install the operating layer that carries the mission through every program, every location, and every staff turnover. Documented, transferable, survivable. So the twentieth family feels the same standard as the first.
