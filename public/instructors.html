The instructors

Three voices. One curriculum. All in your corner.

Every mentee in the TOJ Advisory cohort works with three instructors — academic readiness, NIL gameplan, financial literacy. Each one has real classroom time with student-athletes at real programs. Nobody teaches from theory here.

Weeks 1–2 · Academic + College Readiness

Carly Notaroberto

The peer voice for student-athletes
University track record
Georgia State · Delaware · USF
Sports covered
All sports · all teams
Classroom experience
Has led college courses for student-athletes

Carly has spent her career inside the student-athlete life at Division I programs. At Georgia State, she supported student-athletes across every sport on the roster. At Delaware, she built out academic support systems that traveled across every team. She’s now at the University of South Florida, still working directly with the athletes who show up on the field Saturday and in class Monday.

Her track record isn’t just administrative — she’s led college courses designed for student-athletes. She knows what actually lands in a room where every person has a game to prepare for. She teaches what she’s already taught.

What she leads in the cohort

  • College eligibility · what it actually means (NCAA / NAIA clearinghouses)
  • Division levels · FBS / FCS / D1 / D2 / D3 / NAIA / JUCO
  • Academic minimums · GPA / test scores / course requirements
  • Balancing sport, school, and life · time management for real
  • Intro to college · dorms, classes, being on your own

Kyron Cumby

The NIL Gameplan Architect · strategic + compliance backbone
University track record
University of Illinois · Delaware

Founder, Trail of Joy Player Management Group

Kyron built Trail of Joy Player Management Group around a single conviction — the athlete is not the product, they are the subject. That reframe is what led to the six-role Core Agent Stack, the Operational Transcript, and the standard that now sits behind every TOJ deliverable across all four avenues.

His track record spans the University of Illinois and Delaware, where he worked directly with athletes on positioning, contract literacy, and the strategic side of NIL. When a mentee needs help reading a brand deal before signing, understanding a collective’s valid business purpose under the 2026 rules, or shaping a personal brand that lasts past their last game — that’s the room he owns.

What he leads in the cohort

  • What NIL actually means · rule map
  • High school vs. college NIL · collectives · schools
  • NIL contracts · key terms · what to sign
  • Film discipline · Player Transcript · coach outreach
  • Brand positioning · content execution · social audit

Weeks 5–6 · Financial Literacy + NIL Money

Nicole Bradley

The Money Coach Behind the Athletes
Track record
Gonzaga · NFL · Big Ten
Scope
All sports · all athletes
Focus
Taxes · budgeting · red-flag awareness

Nicole is the financial literacy anchor of the SubjectSkillz funnel — the voice parents actually trust when the topic is money. Her track record spans Gonzaga, the NFL, and Big Ten programs, working with athletes across every sport and every stage of income.

She teaches what most athletes learn only after they’ve made a mistake — taxes on NIL income, the 25–30% rule, quarterly estimates, budgeting habits that outlast the deal that made them necessary, and how to spot the predatory advisor before the contract gets signed. If Weeks 1–4 are about earning the deal, Weeks 5–6 are about keeping the money the deal produced.

What she leads in the cohort

  • NIL income is taxable · the 25–30% rule
  • Quarterly estimated taxes
  • Contracts & payment records · keeping receipts
  • Budgeting · saving · red-flag awareness
  • Signing day · what you’re actually agreeing to
