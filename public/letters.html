Letter No. 04 · Sunday

The regulars aren't leaving because you're bad.

Ran the retention numbers for three small operators this week. Two of them thought they had a service problem. They didn't. They had a silence problem — the gap between a customer's last visit and the next reason to come back was too long, and something else filled it.

What we're testing: a weekly, one-line touchpoint tied to something the customer actually did last time. Not a promo. Not a newsletter. A specific breadcrumb back to the relationship. Early read: reactivation up, unsubscribes flat.

What you can use: before you spend a dollar on new customers this month, count how many days pass on average between your regulars' visits — and whether anything from you shows up in that gap. If the answer is nothing, that's the leak.

— Kyron
