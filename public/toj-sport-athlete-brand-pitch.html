TOJ Sport · Department Vision & Strategy

Athlete Brand & NIL.

"Built from who they are. Sold on who they become."

00 · ESSENCE

The lens everything below runs through

Every section in this document is a practical instrument — a content calendar, a partnership pitch, a metrics review. None of it is the point. The point is underneath it: the thing a family can't see on a deck but feels in the first phone call.

We operate within who we actually are — not a persona built for the reel. We operate from a point of integrity — what's in the deck is what's said on the phone is what's said to the athlete's face. And we're inclined toward good by default, not by campaign — that's the difference between a referral and an ad. Essence-level work is what produces referrals. Everything else here is downstream of that one fact.

Where it shows up: every advocacy call, every honest film read, every partnership pitch that turns down a check that doesn't fit, every dollar this department sells.
01

Brand Foundation

What the athlete gets: clarity, before anyone else has to ask for it — a name recruiters, brands, and family already trust before they've met the athlete in person.

The foundation is not built. It's found. The athlete already has a story — the way they train, the way they talk about their teammates, the thing they won't compromise on. The job is to name it precisely enough that it survives a bad Saturday. A brand invented from nothing collapses the first off week. A brand built from something true just keeps being true.

02

Visual & Verbal Identity

What the athlete gets: an identity that doesn't need rebuilding when they move levels — high school to college, college to the league.

Logo or monogram, color palette, tone of voice, a short tagline, a one-line mission, a style guide that travels across every platform. The test for every design decision: does this still make sense when they're wearing a different jersey. Tone of voice comes from Essence, not a swipe file — the tagline is the sentence the athlete already says about themselves, tightened.

03

Digital Strategy

Sport-agnostic. Football is TOJ's flagship, not its ceiling — this section runs the same for basketball, baseball, softball, track, volleyball, wrestling, any sport with a recruiting or NIL market behind it.

Social media presence

Platform selection, posting cadence, and the metrics that predict a partnership deal — engagement rate and audience quality, not raw follower count.

Content buckets

Sport (the work), lifestyle (the person), community (the impact). Every post belongs to one of the three; if it doesn't, it doesn't ship.

What the athlete gets: a body of consistent, dated proof — the paper trail that makes the Section 05 partnership pitch credible.
04

Media & PR Strategy

Media training — prepared, not performed. Publicity calendar — built around real dates already on the calendar: season openers, signing day, awards, the offseason. Press kit — bio, stats, media-ready photos, highlight footage, prior coverage, contact info, sent in under sixty seconds when a reporter or brand asks.

What the athlete gets: the ability to be quoted correctly the first time, every time.
05

Brand Partnerships, NIL & Business Opportunities

This is where Sections 01–04 turn into revenue. Sport-agnostic — the NIL market runs across every level and every sport this department touches.

Partnership criteria

Alignment with the athlete's actual brand pillars first, dollar amount second. A brand that shares the athlete's values, gives creative room, and reaches the athlete's real audience compounds. A check that pays and disappears doesn't.

Pitch strategy — one logic track, five emotional tracks

A partnership isn't decided on a spreadsheet — it's decided on how the deck makes the reader feel, then justified with the spreadsheet after.

Logic

The infrastructure case

Evaluation, production, and distribution already run under one roof. Verifiable, not promised — what gets a deal through approval.

01

Becoming

Sells the trajectory, not the snapshot. A partner buys the story of getting there early.

02

Status

Association is worth something on its own. Being in this circle is part of what's being sold.

03

Certainty

Every claim backed by a receipt — a stat, a clip, a delivery record. No adjective asked to stand alone.

04

Anticipation

Points forward — the season, the next level, the next drop. What's coming outweighs what already happened.

05

Cost of standing still

The real, time-limited cost of waiting — a season, a draft class, a recruiting cycle. Not manufactured urgency.

The rule: show, don't tell. A deck that says "rising star" asks the reader to trust an adjective. A deck that shows the chart, the clip, and the schedule asks the reader to trust their own eyes. Sell the thing we built, not a description of it.

How every pitch behaves

The six tracks above are what the pitch says. These five rules govern how it moves.

01

Prevent the worse

Framed as protection, not persuasion — what's actually at risk if this window closes. Real risk, never manufactured.

02

Move to action

Every pitch ends with the next real step, never "think it over." A feeling with no action attached is entertainment.

03

Feel before they understand

The emotional tracks lead the read. Logic closes the deal — it doesn't open it.

04

Change how they think

Attention stops feeling like something that just happens and becomes the asset being built on purpose.

05

Serve to create love

Genuine, unasked-for service — not just satisfaction — is what turns a client into someone who refers without being asked.

Endorsement tiers

A tiered model — legacy brands, emerging brands, niche or local brands — building a portfolio that reflects how the athlete actually lives. Where possible, a paid partnership pairs with real product use, because a deal that isn't true to Essence doesn't survive past the first payment.
