The value ladder

Eight rungs. From lead gen to authority.

Every service TOJ Advisory delivers stacks on the one below it. Foundation is at the bottom. Authority is the peak. Each rung is a job the standard actually performs — not a marketing promise.

01

Foundation

Who you are, what you do, what you stand for. The identity work that has to be in place before the rest of the ladder means anything.

02

Framework

The structure you work inside — positioning, brand system, editorial voice, session cadence. What turns effort into repeatable output.

03

Practice Reps

The actual work — bookings, shoots, features, published pieces. Not talent claims — a record of shipped output against the standard.

04

Documentation

Portfolio, credential log, quarterly Transcript. The proof-of-work trail that turns "I do this" into "here's the receipt."

05

Visibility

Getting real audiences — coaches, recruiters, sponsors, peers — to see the athlete, the trainer, the results. Not just reach; the right eyes.

06

Community

The peer roster: athletes, trainers, and creators who lift each other. The Athletes Certified ecosystem is not a list — it's a network that generates its own opportunities.

07

Brand

Positioning each participant with a durable identity — not just a nickname or a highlight reel. What people say when the athlete or trainer isn't in the room.

08

Authority

The apex rung. Becoming the room others check before deciding — the trainer other trainers cite, the athlete other athletes name-drop, the report scouts trust by default.

Every mentee on TOJ Advisory has all eight rungs running underneath — even when the client only asks for one.

Who TOJ Advisory serves

Four archetypes. One standard.

If you're building your own brand — as an individual, not an operation — TOJ Advisory is your front door. What differs is which product configuration you enter through — not the standard, and not the transcript.

Archetype 01

Young Sports Creators

Camera operators, editors, on-field storytellers building a personal brand around sports media. The formal SubjectSkillz path.

Archetype 02

Athletes Building Their Own Brand

Athletes with a story, a following, or an ambition beyond the roster — who need brand coaching, not full-operations management.

Archetype 03

Emerging Media Figures

Podcasters, analysts, personality-led creators in and around sport. Personal brand at the center; TOJ Advisory as the framework behind the growth.

Archetype 04

Personal Brand Builders

Individuals whose brand is themselves — figures who need structure, cadence, and honest feedback more than they need a full ops team.
