## Purpose of this memo

This memo formalizes what we've already agreed to verbally. It is not a contract — that's counsel's job after both sides say yes to what's here. This is the operator-level document that lets the owner send it to counsel with one line: *send it, this is what we're doing.*

Every section that follows is a decision, not a proposal. The goal is that after 20 minutes with this memo, the owner is more certain they were right to say yes than they were before they opened it.

---

## 1. Scope of engagement

TOJ delivers a national partnership across all five Pylon territorial regionals — Northeast, Southeast, Midwest, South Central, and West — plus international events (Pylon Japan March 6–7, 2027 as the pilot), Pylon City Leagues franchise support (year-round nationally), and the Pylon Girls Flag circuit (LA28-runway compounding vertical).

Term begins with the 2026 season and runs through the 2027 competitive year, auto-renewing annually unless either party gives 60 days notice.

---

## 2. Operating presence

Kyron (TOJ) is physically present in Dallas and the West Coast — Portland is his 2026–2027 base, Seattle secondary — for the initial term. A Northeast relocation is planned for 2028 aligned to his St John's Queens transition.

This presence map informs the Regional Coordinator hire sequence in §7 below. Territories where Kyron is present get earliest, deepest onboarding support. Territories where he's remote get Coordinator + Subject Media contract crew coverage from day one, so no region is under-served.

---

## 3. The relationship shape

Pylon contracts TOJ. Kyron is not W-2 to Pylon. He operates through TOJ under this partnership. That structure is intentional — it preserves TOJ's ability to build the operating IP that scales this partnership and, over time, becomes distributable to other verticals.

TOJ sits **above** Pylon's existing infrastructure. Sports Thread runs the rankings. Team Inn runs the housing. Jordan Brand, Wilson, Muscle Milk, Battle, Overtime, BallerTV, and Whistle are the top-tier sponsors already booked with multi-year renewals and category exclusivity. All of that stays as it is. TOJ builds nothing parallel.

What TOJ builds is the layer downstream that Pylon corp doesn't have bandwidth to reach one-to-one across 1,261 teams, 21,330 spectator tickets, 38+ states, and 117,904 athletes: production, mid-market sponsor sales, athlete-side pipeline, content operations, and the connective tissue that makes every SKU below deliverable at national scale.

**North star, in one line:** *save Pylon time, produce Pylon revenue.* Every SKU in §5 either saves Pylon labor hours or opens a revenue line Pylon isn't monetizing today. Anything that does neither doesn't ship.

## 4. Three operational domains TOJ owns

**Domain 1 — Scouting.** Digital Scouts on-field grading crews at every Pylon event, Subject Report athlete profiles at scale, integration with Optimum grading, feeder to Sports Thread's ranking pipeline, and Recruiting Concierge (the outreach agent that answers college coaches on athletes' behalf, backed by Pylon film and Optimum grades).

**Domain 2 — Creative.** Subject Media production of every Team Media Package, real-time content posting during games (the Live Event Console), per-athlete hype content, sponsor-integrated content, and the four content pillars (Ritual · Scale · Prestige · Capture) that carry the Pylon brand across the calendar. Content authored on TOJ-built infrastructure, produced under Pylon's voice, approved by a Pylon-hired Creative Lead.

**Domain 3 — Efficiency.** TOJ automation of every sales sequence pre/during/post event, CRM nurture, package upsell flows, sponsor fulfillment reporting, merch drop operations, and the City League Host Toolkit that supports the franchise rollout.

Efficiency includes a Regional Coordinator layer — one Coordinator per Pylon territorial regional (five total in the US, one for international) — that handles local Digital Scouts crew management, City League host relationships, vendor booth operations, and post-event follow-up. Regional Coordinators are the connective tissue that makes national coverage physically deliverable without Kyron traveling to every event.

Kyron's scope across all three domains covers **four functions**: Tech, Sales, Scouting, and Media. He is the accountable operator; the Creative Lead runs day-to-day media execution on top of the TOJ system.

## 6. Content Taxonomy — the deliverable spec

Every event produces at minimum **six pieces per team**: 1 POV Reel (per selected star athlete) + 1 Action Photos Carousel + 3 Support Content pieces (team highlight + single shots + individual focus). Season Pack (SKU #1b) × 5+ events = 30+ pieces per team per season.

Visual Direction is codified as the QC standard: cinematic realism, shallow depth of field, high-contrast edits, candid emotion, dynamic body positioning. Any Pylon Media asset that doesn't meet the bar goes back to the creator with specific rubric feedback. Consistency is the moat.

## 7. Regional Coordinator hire sequence

Coordinators onboard in the order Kyron can physically train and validate them. This isn't scheduling — it's operating-integrity. The earliest hires catch the operational issues before they templatize; later hires inherit a working playbook.

1. **West Coordinator (Portland)** — Q1 2026, colocated with Kyron for onboarding depth. Anchors Pacific Northwest event coverage and the Nike/Intel/Portland State sponsor pipeline.
2. **South Central Coordinator (Dallas)** — Q1 2026, colocated with Kyron during Dallas swings. Anchors the TAPPS pathway and Hooks-adjacent content.
3. **Northeast Coordinator (Philly or NY)** — Q2 2026, remote initially, transitions to Kyron-adjacent when he relocates to Queens in 2028. Anchors NY/NJ/PA/DE/DC + SEC/ACC/Ivy scout adjacency.
4. **Southeast Coordinator (Atlanta or Tampa)** — Q3 2026, remote, supported by Subject Media contract crew at events. Anchors the SEC talent pool and defends against Battle Sports.
5. **Midwest Coordinator (Chicago or Indianapolis)** — Q4 2026 OR deferred to Q1 2027 pending year-one cash-flow visibility. Blue-collar football markets.
6. **International Coordinator (Tokyo — Pylon Japan pilot)** — contract-only for the March 2027 event; permanent hire dependent on international expansion volume.

## 8. Regional Coordinator compensation

**Retainer + performance-based commission**, both anchored to Pylon's north-star: drive revenue for Pylon and save the owner's time.

**Retainer:** monthly base, sized to make the role a real full-time or high-commitment part-time engagement for the Coordinator. Amount set jointly with Pylon CEO before the first Q1 2026 hire.

**Commission:** percentage on Coordinator's-territory-attributable revenue — Team Media Package sales, athlete subscription conversions, mid-market sponsor deals under the Kyron approval threshold, City League Host Toolkit closes, Player Package sales at vendor booths in-territory. Percentage set jointly with Pylon CEO.

**Owner-time saved is a metric.** Coordinators are measured not only on gross revenue attributed but on incidents-not-escalated-to-Kyron and hours-saved-on-Pylon-corp-side. Both parties agree on the specific metrics that constitute "owner time saved" before the first Coordinator posting goes live. Weight of the metric in commission calculation set jointly with CEO.

**Governance:** Regional Coordinators are TOJ contractors, not Pylon employees. All Coordinator contracts, IP, and personnel-relationship retention stay with TOJ. This preserves TOJ's operational IP portability across future partnerships. Pylon has no claim to Coordinator contracts on partnership termination.

## 9. Integration with Sports Thread and Team Inn

Sports Thread's ranking coverage extends to all five territorial regionals. TOJ's grade + stat data feed **enriches** ranking accuracy in every territory without altering Sports Thread's editorial authority in any of them. Rankings authorship stays with Sports Thread. TOJ produces content that references and amplifies rankings — never claims to author them.

Team Inn is Pylon's official housing partner for Stay-to-Play weekends. Every TOJ-produced event page, team media package, and family-facing communication surfaces the Team Inn booking flow. TOJ does not build a parallel housing product and never routes families outside the Team Inn flow.

Both partnerships are honored, protected, and promoted throughout the term.

## 10. Optimum sub-agreement

Optimum signs a companion sub-agreement formalizing its 20–50% shares on graded SKUs (lines 1c, 2, 3, 9-Combine, and 11). **No teams are billed on any graded SKU until Optimum's sub-agreement is signed.**

This gates the launch of the Combine Book (1c), Team Analytics (2), Digital Scouts (3), Combine-tier subscription (9), and Recruiting Concierge (11) — the highest-value scouting-side lines. Kyron leads the Optimum conversation on TOJ's behalf; Pylon owner is copied on progress.

The TAPPS cross-recruit pathway (Optimum's official partnership with the ~250 Texas private/parochial programs) becomes a South Central-region growth channel once the sub-agreement is signed. South Central Coordinator (Dallas) is the operational owner of TAPPS outreach after Kyron's initial Optimum pitch.

## 11. Subject Media crew allocation

- **Mentorship crew** — event-level, included by default in Recap (1a), Season Pack (1b), and Live Event Posting (10). Trains younger creators on Pylon events.
- **Contract crew** — premium, event-contracted or year-contracted. Assigned to Combine Book (1c), Digital Scouts (3), high-value events, and all international events (Pylon Japan).

Player-level packages (12/13/14) draw from the Coordinator's region-assigned creator pool, with contract crew callable for Player Feature ($199) when scale requires.

## 12. Customer-facing brand

**Pylon is the only customer-facing brand.** No "Subject Report" or "Subject Media" logo, mark, name, or reference appears on any team, athlete, parent, coach, sponsor, or host-facing surface — including checkout flows, admin UIs the Creative Lead operates, email templates, PDF exports, or sponsor decks. Backend tables and internal skill files may still reference the historical names; customer-facing labels are Pylon-branded end-to-end.

Subject Report and Subject Media remain live inside tojcampaign as TOJ's own proof-of-concept portfolio, used to close future non-Pylon partnerships. They are TOJ's showreel, not Pylon's products.

## 15. Exit clause

On termination:

**TOJ retains:** all customer relationships, CRM data, storefront accounts, athlete subscribers, ambassador records, Regional Coordinator contracts, Creative Lead-independent content library (produced by TOJ contractors), and all licensed IP.

**Pylon retains:** event and team-registration data, direct Pylon-corp sponsor relationships (Jordan, Wilson, Muscle Milk, Battle, Overtime, BallerTV, Whistle), the Sports Thread and Team Inn partnerships, and the Pylon brand marks.

**Regional Coordinators** are TOJ contractors and remain TOJ personnel on termination.

**Sports Thread and Team Inn partnerships** stay Pylon's regardless of TOJ termination.

Both parties agree that a graceful exit is more valuable than any contested asset. Cooperation clauses (data handoff timelines, customer-communication scripts, delivery-in-flight completion) will be spelled out by counsel.
