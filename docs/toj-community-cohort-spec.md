# TOJ Community + Cohort Spec

**Companion to:** `docs/ai-notes-implementation-plan.md` (v3)
**Last updated:** 2026-07-18
**Purpose:** the community-and-cohort layer the notes describe as the retention floor for TOJ's D2C future. Currently missing entirely. Designed against Mazell's hierarchy — belonging + identity ("Do I belong / Who am I?") — because those are the emotional needs that keep an operator subscribed after the first-year value degrades.

---

## The problem this solves

TOJcampaign's concierge tiers deliver value 1:1. That value peaks in months 2–5 and gradually diminishes as the client absorbs the framework. Without a **belonging + status layer** underneath, the client's rational move at month 12 is to churn. That's not a TOJ product problem — that's a TOJ operating-model problem.

The cohort + community layer answers two questions the concierge relationship alone doesn't:

1. **Do I belong here?** — do other operators like me build this way? Do they take me seriously?
2. **Who am I?** — inside this system, do I have a place, a status, a role that isn't just "paying subscriber"?

Answer both, and the client's rational move at month 12 shifts. They don't churn — they lean in.

## The cohort model

### Structure

- **Cohort size:** 30–50 operators (small enough to know each other; large enough for a signal)
- **Duration:** 6 weeks (matches `docs/six-week-foundational-playbook.md`)
- **Cadence:** 4 cohorts per year (Feb, May, Aug, Nov) — leaves recovery windows for Kyron + the roster
- **Format:** weekly live session (60 min, Wed 12pm CT), async work in the cohort's private community space, one 1:1 with Concierge Coach per week
- **Delivery mode:** mastery-based (see `docs/toj-vertical-theses.md` § Thesis 2)

### Price

**Cohort seat:** $2,500 for the 6-week program

- Positioning: mid-tier concierge alternative for operators who want group energy + guided delivery, not white-glove 1:1
- Alumni benefit: cohort alumni get lifetime access to the alumni community + first-in-line on all future cohorts at Founding pricing

**Why $2,500 and not tiered:** the cohort IS the tier. It's a discrete offer, not a subscription upsell. Priced high enough to filter for commitment (not tourists), low enough to be accessible to solo operators (below the $499 × 6 mo = $2,994 Premium equivalent).

### Cohort staff

- **Kyron** — facilitates the 6 live sessions (60 min each × 6 = 6 hours of Kyron time per cohort)
- **Concierge Coach agent** — runs the 1:1 weekly check-ins (30 min per operator × 30–50 operators × 6 weeks = 90–150 hours; Coach agent scales this without linear Kyron time)
- **Content Strategist agent** — produces the cohort-specific weekly materials
- **Retention Watcher agent** — flags operators falling behind
- **Cohort Program Owner** — new role, TBD hire (part-time Q1 2027 launch, full-time Q3 2027)

### Success criteria per cohort

- 80%+ completion rate (30-of-40 or 40-of-50 finish all 6 weeks)
- 60%+ NPS
- 40%+ convert to a paid subscription (Founding/Core/Premium) within 60 days of cohort close
- 3+ referrals per cohort to future cohorts by month 6 alumni
- Zero cases where a cohort member's Foundation Score dropped during the cohort (a Foundation Score is supposed to move UP during structured engagement; a drop means the cohort delivered wrong)

### First cohort — Q1 2027

**Sourcing:** existing Premium and Core clients invited first (guaranteed alumni-benefit + first-cohort discount). Fill remaining seats via waitlist.

**Target seat count:** 30 (smallest viable). Expand to 40 in Q2 cohort if Q1 hits completion criteria.

## The all-alumni community space

### Structure

- **Platform:** Skool for v1 (Q1 2027 launch); custom on Next.js stack post-Q3 2027 if it scales past 500 alumni
- **Channels:** #announcements, #weekly-pulse, #pillar-01-brand, ...#pillar-05-growth, #vertical-personal-brand, #vertical-authority, #vertical-non-profit, #alumni-lounge, #wins, #asks
- **Access:** every cohort graduate + every Premium subscriber (Premium tier includes community access) + every SaaS subscriber at "founding operator" pricing tier

### Weekly rhythm

- **Monday** — Chief of Staff cross-posts the platform's weekly pulse (what shipped, what's new, what the roster observed across pilots — anonymized)
- **Wednesday** — Kyron drops a 5-minute video (async, not live) — one operator observation from the week
- **Friday** — Ask thread ("what's the ONE thing you're stuck on?") — members answer members
- **Monthly** — Live Q&A with Kyron (60 min, first Wed of the month)
- **Quarterly** — Live cohort alumni event (Zoom → in-person once size supports)

### Community operating principles (posted at join)

1. **Respect the vocabulary.** Foundation, Operational Transcript, Non-profit (hyphenated), pillars in canonical order. Corrected gently and consistently.
2. **Specific over abstract.** Every post answers the "what specifically" question. Vague success stories get asked to specify.
3. **No hype comments.** No "🔥" reply chains. Substantive replies only.
4. **No cross-selling.** Operators may reference their own work when it answers a member's question. No pitch decks in DMs. Violation → warning; second violation → removed.
5. **No AI-generated posts posing as human.** Members can share AI-assisted work; must be tagged as such.

## The private events layer

### Q2 2027 first event

- **Format:** 1-day in-person event, single city (Chicago or Austin — CFO decides based on alumni density), 30–50 attendees
- **Content:** 4 hours of Kyron teaching (2 blocks of 2 hours with breaks) + 3 hours of small-group work + 1-hour dinner
- **Price:** $500 for cohort alumni; $1,500 for Premium subscribers not yet cohort alumni
- **Success signal:** 30+ attend, NPS > 70, generates ≥ 10 new cohort/subscription enrollments within 30 days post-event

### Beyond Q2 2027

- 2 per year cadence, alternating cities
- Add vertical-specific mini-events (Authority-vertical retreat at a football program's facilities; Non-profit-vertical convening at a foundation's HQ — sponsorship-friendly)
- Alumni Board convenes at first live event; nominates hosts for future events

## Belonging + identity — the layered promise

### Belonging (Mazell)

The community answers "do I belong here?" by:

- Naming operators by name in the weekly pulse (Chief of Staff surfaces one operator move per week — quiet recognition)
- Vertical channels give operators sub-communities where their language and pain matches
- Cohort experience creates 30–50 people who know each other's businesses at pillar-detail level

### Identity (Mazell)

The community answers "who am I in this?" by:

- Titles that mean something: Founding Operator, Certified Operator (Q3 2027), Cohort Alumni, Alumni Board
- Vested status (see `docs/toj-alumni-benefits.md` when written) — visible longevity + contribution
- Roles inside the community — cohort lead, vertical steward, Ask-thread respondent-of-the-month

### Public / Private / Secret / Shadow selves (from the notes)

The notes name four selves an operator carries — Public (LinkedIn), Private (family + close team), Secret (real fears + real goals), Shadow (what they'd rather not admit).

TOJ's community operates only in the **Public and Private** layers. It never asks the operator to share Secret or Shadow content — those belong to Concierge Coach 1:1s and to Kyron's Premium working sessions. Community members show up as themselves at the Private layer (real about their business, real about their stuck-points) but never at the Secret/Shadow layer (that's not what group settings are for).

This is a **guardrail**, not a limit. Group belonging works best when the layer of self shared is bounded correctly. Going too deep in group breaks trust; staying too shallow breaks intimacy.

Add to `agents/skills/operator-self-audit.md`: the 4-selves distinction is a scaffold for Concierge Coach conversations, not community posts.
